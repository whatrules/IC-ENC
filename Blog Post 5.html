INAHINA visit IC-ENC UK Office

by Kevin Black at 11:36 in Technical


​INAHINA, the National Institute of Hydrography and Navigation of Mozambique, visited IC-ENC this week to discuss their ENC production plans.

×

Left to right: Richard Fowle, IC-ENC Technical Manager Augusto Jessenao Bata, General Director Humberto Raul Mutevuie, Director of Hydrography and Cartography Service Celia Margarida Magaia, Head of the Cartographic Department Susana Rouque Tembe, Head of the Foreign affairs Department James Harper, IC-ENC General Manager

INAHINA is a long standing member of IC-ENC and is now developing its own national ENC production and maintenance programme.

IC-ENC will be supporting INAHINA’s ambitions via its ENC Production Support service and the IC-ENC ENC Validation training course.

The IC-ENC ENC Validation training course has been developed by IC-ENC’s technical experts. It is designed to train its members’ ENC production departments in all aspects of ENC validation without favouring any one piece of validation software. The pilot course will run in March for selected delegates and be available for other delegates from amongst the IC-ENC membership during 2014. For further details relating to this course please contact Mike Hawes, IC-ENC Data Manager.

Mission:
IC-ENC aims to provide services, at a low cost, to national Hydrographic Offices that ensure their ENCs are compliant to the international standards, consistent across the global dataset, and readily available for use. This is so that shipping can navigate safely, efficiently and confidently, whilst ensuring other maritime users are using the same approved data.
